ConvDepth: Self-Supervised Monocular Depth Estimation for Autonomous Driving

Wei Xiong1, Yibo Chen1, Jie Li1, Long Chen2, Qin Zou3, Song Wang4
1Hubei University of Technology, 2Institute of Automation Chinese Academy of Sciences, 3Wuhan University, 4University of South Carolina
Code

Abstract

The utilization of geometric constraints in unstructured monocular videos has demonstrated promising performance in self-supervised monocular depth estimation (MDE). Recent research efforts have focused on addressing the scene hypothesis problem to enhance the accuracy of MDE, which is of paramount importance for various real-world applications, including autonomous driving, robotics, and augmented reality.

In this study, we investigate the impact of advanced networks on MDE performance and propose a deep network architecture, ConvDepth, explicitly tailored to capture underlying patterns and structures within a scene, particularly suited for depth estimation. Additionally, we introduce a robust self-distillation loss function that combines self-distillation loss with robustness loss to provide accurate supervision for network learning.

Numerous experimental results demonstrate that the proposed ConvDepth outperforms other existing techniques by generating more detailed and fine-grained depth maps while extracting comprehensive global information. State-of-the-art results are achieved on the KITTI, Make3D, and Cityscapes benchmark datasets for self-supervised MDE. The ConvDepth source code is publicly available at https://github.com/convdepth/ConvDepth.
